Goal: Model personal savings growth over 24 months to evaluate the feasibility of reaching a €10,000 target for purchasing a car under different retention scenarios.

Role: Process Analyst responsible for building a savings projection model, defining assumptions, and evaluating optimistic, realistic, and pessimistic outcomes.

Tools: Spreadsheet modeling (e.g., Microsoft Excel); scenario-based forecasting; retention coefficient modeling to simulate savings consistency; monthly cash flow calculations.

Key Results: Starting with €1,000 in savings and a monthly accumulation potential of €350, the model projects savings growth across 24 months under three retention scenarios: 0.95 (optimistic), 0.80 (realistic), and 0.60 (pessimistic).
The model estimates whether the €10,000 target is achievable within the defined timeframe and highlights the sensitivity of the outcome to savings discipline (retention rate).
Provides a structured financial roadmap and clear visibility into risk levels associated with inconsistent saving behavior.
